HALLOWEEN HISTORY

Halloween, celebrated on October 31st, has roots tracing back over 2,000 years to the ancient Celtic festival of Samhain. It marked the end of the harvest and the beginning of winter — a time believed to blur the boundary between the worlds of the living and the dead. People lit bonfires and wore costumes to ward off spirits. Over time, Halloween evolved through Christian influences into All Hallows’ Eve and eventually became the fun, spooky holiday we know today — filled with trick-or-treating, haunted houses, and carved pumpkins!
